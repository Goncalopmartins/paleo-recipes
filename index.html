Quiet Paleo Recipes

Dr. Georgia Ede — Change Your Diet, Change Your Mind  ·  9 recipes, each scaled to 2 meals
Key rules: All fresh meat, poultry, seafood and eggs permitted. Fats: extra virgin olive oil, unrefined avocado oil, duck fat, tallow, lard. Seasonings: salt, black pepper, leafy/floral herbs only — no nightshade spices (no paprika, cayenne, chili powder). Alliums must be thoroughly cooked. Crucifers cooked only, max 1 portion/day. Avoid: grains, dairy, legumes, seed oils, refined carbs, sugar, alcohol.
Lemony chicken breasts with asparagus
Prep 10 min
Cook 30 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Oven + stove
Ingredients
Chicken breasts4 pieces (~800g)
Asparagus500g
Extra virgin olive oil4 tbsp
Lemons2 (juice + zest)
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 190°C (fan 170°C). Butterfly or pound chicken breasts to an even 2–2.5cm thickness. Pat dry and season generously on both sides.
2
Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in an oven-safe pan over medium-high. Sear presentation-side down for 4–5 minutes until deep golden. Flip.
3
Squeeze juice of 1 lemon over chicken, add zest, drizzle extra olive oil on top. Transfer to oven and roast 18–22 minutes until juices run clear. Rest 5 minutes before slicing.
4
Snap woody ends off asparagus. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a pan over medium. Cook asparagus 5–7 minutes turning occasionally. Squeeze remaining lemon over.
5
Plate sliced chicken with asparagus, scatter parsley, drizzle pan juices.
Storage: Up to 3 days. Reheat gently in a pan with a splash of olive oil. Slice just before eating to retain moisture.
Pan-seared salmon with zucchini & avocado
Prep 8 min
Cook 15 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Stove only
Ingredients
Salmon fillets4 fillets (~700g)
Zucchini3 medium
Avocados2 ripe
Unrefined avocado oil3 tbsp
Lemon1 (juice)
Fresh parsley or dillsmall handful
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Pat salmon dry, season both sides. Slice zucchini into 1cm half-moons.
2
Heat 1 tbsp avocado oil over medium-high. Cook zucchini in a single layer 4–5 minutes until golden. Season and set aside.
3
Wipe pan, heat 2 tbsp avocado oil over medium-high. Place salmon skin-side down, press gently 30 seconds. Cook 4 minutes without moving. Flip and cook 2–3 more minutes.
4
Slice avocados. Plate salmon with zucchini and avocado. Squeeze lemon juice, finish with herbs and olive oil.
Storage: Up to 2 days. Slice avocado fresh for the second meal — browns if stored cut.
Beef steaks with mushroom, asparagus & sweet potato
Prep 8 min
Cook 25 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Stove only
Ingredients
Beef steaks (sirloin)2 × 200g
Sweet potatoes2 medium
Mushrooms300g
Asparagus1 bunch (~400g)
Extra virgin olive oil3 tbsp
Lemon1 (juice)
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Peel sweet potatoes, cut into 4cm chunks, boil in salted water 15–18 minutes until tender. Drain.
2
Take steaks out of fridge 15 minutes before cooking. Pat dry, season generously on both sides.
3
Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a heavy pan over high until very hot. Sear steaks 2.5–3 minutes per side for medium. Press edges briefly. Remove and rest 5 minutes.
4
Same pan, medium-high, 2 tbsp olive oil. Cook sliced mushrooms 3–4 minutes until browned. Snap woody ends off asparagus, cut into 4–5cm diagonal pieces, add to pan and stir-fry together 3–4 more minutes until asparagus is tender with a slight bite. Squeeze lemon juice over.
5
Plate each steak with the mushroom and asparagus mix and sweet potato. Drizzle resting juices and finish with parsley.
Storage: Store steaks whole and unsliced up to 2 days. Vegetables and sweet potato keep 3 days.
Baked frittata with carrots, spinach & herbs
Prep 10 min
Cook 30 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Oven + stove
Ingredients
Eggs8 large
Carrots3 medium
Fresh spinach150g
Duck fat (or olive oil)2 tbsp
Fresh dill (or parsley)small handful
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Lemon½ (juice)
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 180°C (fan 160°C). Peel carrots and cut into thin rounds (3–4mm).
2
Melt duck fat in an oven-safe pan (22–24cm) over medium. Cook carrots with salt and pepper 6–7 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
3
Add spinach in batches, stir 2–3 minutes until fully wilted and excess water evaporated.
4
Beat eggs with salt, pepper, dill and half the parsley. Pour evenly over vegetables. Cook on stove over low 3–4 minutes until edges set.
5
Transfer to oven and bake 15–18 minutes until fully set and lightly golden. Skewer should come out clean.
6
Cool 5 minutes, squeeze lemon juice, scatter parsley, slice into wedges. Serve warm or at room temperature.
Storage: Up to 3 days. Excellent cold — no reheating needed.
Stuffed portobello mushrooms with minced beef & vegetables
Prep 15 min
Cook 35 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Oven + stove
Ingredients
Large portobello mushrooms4 large
Minced beef400g
Leek1 medium
Carrots2 medium
Zucchini1 medium
Extra virgin olive oil3 tbsp
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Lemon1 (juice)
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 190°C (fan 170°C). Remove mushroom stalks, set caps aside. Finely chop stalks and reserve for filling.
2
Finely dice leek, carrots, zucchini and mushroom stalks into very small pieces — the smaller the better so they cook into the meat.
3
Heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium. Cook leek 5–6 minutes until completely soft with no bite — leek is an allium and must be thoroughly cooked. Add carrots and zucchini, cook 4–5 more minutes.
4
Add minced beef, breaking it up well. Cook over medium-high 6–7 minutes until fully browned. Add mushroom stalks, cook 2 more minutes. Season well, remove from heat, stir in parsley and lemon juice.
5
Place caps gill-side up on a baking tray. Brush outside with remaining 1 tbsp olive oil, season lightly. Spoon filling in generously, pressing and mounding on top.
6
Bake 18–20 minutes until caps are tender and filling is hot through. Finish with parsley and a drizzle of olive oil.
Storage: Up to 3 days. Reheat in oven at 180°C for 10–12 minutes. Avoid microwave.
Pan-fried hake with spinach & warm olives
Prep 8 min
Cook 15 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Stove only
Ingredients
Hake fillets4 fillets (~700g)
Fresh spinach200g
Pitted olives100g
Extra virgin olive oil4 tbsp
Lemons2 (juice)
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Pat hake fillets very dry — hake has high water content. Season both sides with salt and black pepper.
2
Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat. Add spinach in batches and toss 2–3 minutes until fully wilted and excess water evaporated. Season lightly, remove and set aside.
3
Wipe pan, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium-high. Place hake skin-side down. Do not move. Cook 3–4 minutes until flesh is opaque two-thirds up.
4
Carefully flip with a wide spatula and cook 1–2 more minutes. Handle gently — hake breaks apart easily. Remove and rest briefly.
5
Same pan over low, add ½ tbsp olive oil and warm olives 1–2 minutes with a squeeze of lemon. Just warm through, do not fry.
6
Plate hake on a bed of spinach with the warm olives alongside. Squeeze more lemon over everything, finish with parsley and a drizzle of olive oil.
Storage: Best eaten fresh. If storing, refrigerate up to 2 days. Reheat hake very gently in a covered pan with a splash of olive oil; eat the spinach cold or at room temperature.
Beef meatballs with mushrooms
Prep 15 min
Cook 25 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Oven + stove
Ingredients
Minced beef500g
Mushrooms400g
Leek (white part only)½ medium
Egg1 large
Extra virgin olive oil3 tbsp
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Fresh thymea few sprigs
Lemon1 (juice)
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 200°C (fan 180°C). Finely chop leek and cook in 1 tbsp olive oil over medium 5–6 minutes until completely soft. Let cool a few minutes.
2
Combine minced beef, cooked leek, egg, chopped parsley, thyme leaves, salt and pepper. Mix with hands until evenly combined. Do not overwork.
3
Shape into golf-ball sized meatballs (~14–16 total). Place on a lightly oiled baking tray with space between each.
4
Brown meatballs in a pan with 1 tbsp olive oil over high, 2 minutes per side to seal. Transfer to oven and bake 12–15 minutes until cooked through.
5
While meatballs bake, heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a pan over medium-high. Slice mushrooms and cook 4–5 minutes without stirring until deeply browned. Season with salt, pepper and a squeeze of lemon. Scatter with thyme leaves.
6
Serve meatballs on top of the mushrooms, drizzle with olive oil and scatter with parsley.
Storage: Up to 3 days. Reheat meatballs in a pan with olive oil 4–5 minutes turning occasionally, or in oven at 180°C for 10 minutes. Reheat mushrooms briefly in a hot pan.
Shrimp stir-fry with asparagus
Prep 10 min
Cook 12 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Stove only
Ingredients
Small shrimps, peeled500g
Asparagus2 bunches (~700g)
Extra virgin olive oil3 tbsp
Lemons2 (juice + zest)
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Fresh thymea few sprigs
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Pat shrimps dry, season with salt and pepper. Snap woody ends off asparagus and cut into 4–5cm diagonal pieces.
2
Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a wide pan or wok over high heat. Stir-fry asparagus for 4–5 minutes until tender with a slight bite. Season with salt and remove from pan.
3
Return pan to high heat with 2 tbsp olive oil. Cook shrimps in a single layer in batches — 1–1.5 minutes per side. Stop the moment they turn pink and curl. Do not overcook or they turn rubbery.
4
Return asparagus to pan with the shrimps. Add lemon juice, zest and thyme leaves. Toss over high heat for 30 seconds.
5
Plate immediately. Squeeze more lemon, scatter parsley, drizzle olive oil.
Storage: Best eaten fresh. Shrimps turn rubbery when reheated — eat the second meal cold with extra lemon and olive oil as a salad-style dish.
Baby squid stir-fry with spinach & avocado
Prep 10 min
Cook 10 min
Serves 2 meals
Method Stove only
Ingredients
Baby squid, cleaned600g
Fresh spinach150g
Avocados2 ripe
Extra virgin olive oil3 tbsp
Lemons2 (juice)
Fresh parsleysmall handful
Salt & black pepperto taste
Steps
1
Pat baby squid very dry with kitchen paper — moisture prevents a good sear. If whole, separate tentacles from bodies. Season with salt and pepper just before cooking, not earlier — salt draws out moisture.
2
Slice the avocados and arrange on the plates ready to serve. Avocado goes on cold, not in the pan.
3
Heat 1.5 tbsp olive oil in a wide pan or wok over the highest heat possible until just beginning to smoke. Add spinach and toss quickly 1–2 minutes until wilted. Season lightly and remove to a plate.
4
Return pan to highest heat with remaining 1.5 tbsp olive oil. Add squid in a single layer — cook in two batches if needed. Sear without moving 1–1.5 minutes until golden underneath, then toss for another 30–60 seconds. Total time must not exceed 2–3 minutes or squid turns rubbery.
5
Return spinach to pan with squid. Squeeze juice of 1 lemon, toss together over high heat for 20–30 seconds.
6
Plate immediately alongside fresh sliced avocado. Squeeze more lemon over the avocado, drizzle everything with olive oil and scatter with fresh parsley.
Storage: Best eaten immediately. Squid turns rubbery when reheated — eat the second meal cold with extra lemon and olive oil. Do not slice avocado until ready to eat.
